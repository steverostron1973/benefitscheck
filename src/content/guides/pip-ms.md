Multiple sclerosis is one of the conditions most commonly associated with PIP awards — but the type of MS you have, and how it currently affects you, makes a significant difference to your award level. Understanding how the assessment handles both relapsing-remitting and progressive MS is important for getting the right outcome.

## How MS affects PIP — it depends on your current presentation

MS can cause a wide range of symptoms including fatigue, mobility problems, cognitive difficulties, pain, vision problems, bladder and bowel issues, and muscle weakness. Which of these affect you, and how severely, determines your PIP award rather than the diagnosis itself.

## Relapsing-remitting MS and the majority of days rule

For relapsing-remitting MS, the majority of days rule is particularly important. If you experience relapses that significantly affect your functioning for weeks or months at a time, and these occur frequently enough to affect the majority of your days over a 12-month period, they should be fully reflected in your assessment.

Don't describe only how you are in remission. If relapses significantly affect your life for a significant proportion of the year, that's the realistic picture that should be presented.

## How MS typically affects PIP activities

**Daily Living activities often affected:**
- **Preparing food** — fatigue, hand tremor, weakness, and coordination problems can affect cooking significantly
- **Washing and bathing** — fatigue, balance problems, and weakness can make bathing unsafe without adaptations or help
- **Dressing** — weakness, spasticity, and fatigue can make dressing slow and difficult
- **Managing medication** — complex medication regimes including disease-modifying therapies may require support
- **Communicating** — cognitive difficulties and speech problems can affect communication in some people with MS

**Mobility activities often affected:**
- **Moving around** — one of the most commonly affected areas; weakness, spasticity, balance problems, and fatigue can all limit walking distance significantly
- **Planning and following journeys** — cognitive difficulties, fatigue, and uncertainty about symptoms can affect the ability to travel independently

## Fatigue and MS

MS fatigue is different from ordinary tiredness — it can be overwhelming and unpredictable, and it significantly affects the ability to carry out daily activities. Make sure you describe MS fatigue specifically in your claim, including how it affects you throughout the day and how activities earlier in the day affect your ability to do things later.

## What award might you expect?

MS awards vary widely depending on current symptoms. Someone in remission with minimal functional impact may not meet the threshold; someone with significant mobility problems, fatigue, and daily living difficulties may receive Enhanced rate on both components. Progressive MS typically results in higher awards as the condition advances.

## Claiming while working

PIP is unaffected by employment. Many people with MS continue to work, particularly in earlier stages of the condition or during periods of remission, and may still be entitled to PIP based on how MS affects their daily living and mobility.

## What evidence helps your claim

- Neurologist letters describing your MS type, current symptoms, and functional impact
- GP letter supporting your claim
- Evidence of any disease-modifying therapy you're taking
- Occupational therapy assessment if you've had one
- Evidence of any aids, adaptations, or mobility equipment you use
- MS nurse letters if you have one

## Common mistakes when claiming PIP for MS

**Describing only remission** — if you have relapsing-remitting MS, make sure relapses are fully described and their frequency and severity are clear.

**Not mentioning fatigue** — MS fatigue is significant and affects multiple activities but is frequently underreported in claims.

**Not mentioning bladder and bowel symptoms** — these can affect the ability to travel independently and manage personal care, but people often don't mention them out of embarrassment.

## PIP reviews and reassessments

MS is a progressive condition for many people. If your condition worsens between reviews, you can request a reassessment before your scheduled review date. Keep records of any changes in symptoms, new medications, or new aids and adaptations.
