Stroke is one of the leading causes of disability in older people in the UK, and many stroke survivors are entitled to Attendance Allowance without knowing it. The lasting effects of a stroke — physical, cognitive, and emotional — can all affect the care needs that Attendance Allowance is designed to support.

## What is Attendance Allowance?

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free benefit for people over State Pension age who need help with personal care or supervision due to a physical or mental disability. It pays £73.90/week (lower rate) or £110.40/week (higher rate) in 2026/27 and is not means-tested.

## How stroke affects Attendance Allowance eligibility

The effects of a stroke vary enormously depending on which part of the brain was affected and how severe the stroke was. Common lasting effects that affect Attendance Allowance eligibility include:

**Physical effects:**
- Weakness or paralysis on one side of the body (hemiplegia/hemiparesis) affecting the ability to wash, dress, and manage personal care
- Difficulty walking and balance problems increasing fall risk
- Fatigue — post-stroke fatigue is extremely common and can be severe, affecting the ability to manage daily tasks

**Communication effects:**
- Aphasia (difficulty with speaking, understanding, reading, or writing) can affect the ability to communicate care needs and manage daily life
- Dysarthria (slurred speech) can make communication difficult

**Cognitive effects:**
- Memory problems and cognitive impairment affect safety and the ability to manage daily tasks independently
- Executive function difficulties affect planning and decision-making
- Post-stroke dementia can develop in some people

**Emotional effects:**
- Post-stroke depression and anxiety are very common and can significantly affect daily functioning

## When to claim after a stroke

Attendance Allowance can be claimed as soon as the care needs arise — there's no waiting period for physical conditions (unlike PIP, which has a 3-month qualifying period). However, the DWP looks at needs that have lasted or are expected to last for at least 6 months.

If a stroke has left lasting disabilities, claim as soon as the person's care needs are established — don't wait to see how much recovery occurs. If they recover significantly, the award can be reviewed. If they don't, claiming early means receiving the benefit for longer.

## Which rate applies after a stroke?

This depends on the severity of lasting effects. A mild stroke with good recovery may not meet the threshold. A stroke with significant lasting disability affecting both daytime care and night-time needs (for example, needing help to reposition in bed, getting up to use the toilet, or managing distress and confusion at night) may qualify for the higher rate.

## What evidence helps the claim

- GP letter describing the stroke, lasting effects, and current care needs
- Hospital discharge summary
- Stroke consultant or rehabilitation team letters
- Occupational therapy assessment if one has been done
- Speech and language therapy records if communication is affected
- Description of the daily care routine from the main carer

The Stroke Association has a helpline and benefits advice service that can help with the claim.

## What Attendance Allowance unlocks

Attendance Allowance can trigger or increase Pension Credit through the severe disability addition, unlocking Council Tax Reduction, free NHS dental treatment, and other passported benefits.
