Attendance Allowance is one of the most valuable benefits available to older people with dementia — yet it's also one of the most underclaimed. Many families caring for someone with dementia don't know it exists, or assume their relative won't qualify. In reality, dementia almost always qualifies for Attendance Allowance, often at the higher rate.

## What is Attendance Allowance?

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free benefit for people over State Pension age who need help with personal care or supervision due to a physical or mental disability. It's not means-tested — income, savings, and whether you live alone or with others don't affect your entitlement.

There are two rates:
- **Lower rate** (£73.90/week, 2026/27) — for people who need help during the day or during the night
- **Higher rate** (£110.40/week, 2026/27) — for people who need help during both day and night, or who are terminally ill

## How dementia affects Attendance Allowance eligibility

Dementia affects the ability to carry out personal care safely and independently across multiple areas. The key question for Attendance Allowance is whether the person needs help or supervision — either physically helping them or being present to ensure their safety.

Dementia commonly affects:

**Personal care** — washing, bathing, dressing, eating, and using the toilet can all become difficult as dementia progresses. Many people with dementia need prompting, step-by-step guidance, or physical assistance with these tasks.

**Safety supervision** — this is particularly important for dementia. A person with dementia may be at risk from leaving the hob on, wandering, falling, taking wrong medication doses, or not recognising danger. The need for supervision to prevent these risks counts toward Attendance Allowance even if no physical help is needed.

**Night needs** — wandering at night, becoming confused and distressed, needing reassurance, or requiring help with toileting during the night all count toward the higher rate.

## Which rate does dementia usually attract?

Many people with moderate to advanced dementia qualify for the higher rate because their needs span both day and night. Confusion and wandering at night is extremely common in dementia, and this typically means higher rate Attendance Allowance.

Even early-stage dementia may qualify for the lower rate where supervision needs during the day are established.

## Claiming while in a care home

If the person with dementia lives in a care home funded by the local authority, Attendance Allowance is generally not payable. However, if they're self-funding their care home place, they can still receive Attendance Allowance. If they move from home to a care home, Attendance Allowance stops after 28 days if the local authority is contributing to costs.

## What evidence helps the claim

The Attendance Allowance claim form (AA1) asks detailed questions about care needs. For dementia claims:

- Be specific about safety supervision needs — don't just say "they need watching," describe specific risks and incidents
- Describe night-time needs in detail — how often, what happens, how long it takes
- Include a letter from the GP describing the dementia diagnosis and stage
- If there's a memory clinic or specialist involved, their input helps
- A letter from the main carer describing the daily care routine is valuable

## The claim form — who should complete it?

People with moderate to advanced dementia often cannot complete the claim form themselves. A family member, carer, or professional can complete it on their behalf. If the person has a lasting power of attorney in place, the attorney can act for them.

When completing the form, describe the person's needs on their worst days — dementia fluctuates and bad days should be represented, not just the better ones.

## What Attendance Allowance unlocks

Receiving Attendance Allowance also acts as a passport to other benefits. It can trigger entitlement to Pension Credit (or increase an existing Pension Credit award through the severe disability addition), which in turn unlocks further benefits including full Council Tax Reduction and free NHS dental treatment.
