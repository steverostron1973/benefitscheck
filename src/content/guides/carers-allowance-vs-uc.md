If you're a carer on a low income, you may be entitled to both Carer's Allowance and Universal Credit. But the way they interact isn't straightforward — and misunderstanding the overlap rule can lead to confusion about what you'll actually receive.

## The overlapping benefits rule

Carer's Allowance and Universal Credit can be claimed at the same time, but due to the overlapping benefits rule, you won't receive both in full as separate cash payments.

If you're entitled to both, your Carer's Allowance is taken into account as income when calculating your UC award — effectively reducing your UC payment by the same amount. So you don't receive Carer's Allowance on top of UC; it's more like it gets absorbed into the calculation.

## The Carer Element — the real benefit

Here's the important part: if you're entitled to Carer's Allowance (even if you don't receive it as a cash payment due to the overlap), you automatically qualify for the Carer Element of Universal Credit.

The Carer Element adds £198.31 per month to your UC award (2026/27 rate). This is separate from — and in addition to — the standard UC allowance.

So in practice, being entitled to Carer's Allowance while on UC means you get the Carer Element added to your UC, which is often worth more than Carer's Allowance itself (£83.30/week = £361/month vs the Carer Element at £198.31/month as a top-up).

## How to claim both

Claim Universal Credit through GOV.UK in the usual way, and indicate on your claim that you're a carer. You should also make a separate Carer's Allowance claim — this establishes your underlying entitlement, which triggers the Carer Element even if the cash payment is affected by the overlap rule.

## Effect on the person you care for

If the person you care for is also on certain means-tested benefits, being awarded Carer's Allowance can affect their Severe Disability Premium. This is worth checking before claiming if the person you care for receives means-tested benefits.
