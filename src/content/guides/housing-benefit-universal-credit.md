The short answer is: for most working-age people, no — you can't claim both Housing Benefit and Universal Credit at the same time. Housing Benefit has been largely replaced by the housing costs element within Universal Credit. But there are important exceptions.

## Why you generally can't get both

When Universal Credit was introduced, it was designed to replace six legacy benefits including Housing Benefit. If you're of working age and make a new claim for help with rent, you'll receive it through the housing costs element of Universal Credit rather than as a separate Housing Benefit payment.

If you're already on Housing Benefit and you make a new Universal Credit claim, your Housing Benefit will usually stop.

## Who can still claim Housing Benefit separately

There are specific groups who can still claim Housing Benefit outside of Universal Credit:

**Pension age claimants** — if you've reached State Pension age, you claim Housing Benefit directly from your local council rather than through Universal Credit. If you're in a couple and one of you is working age, the rules are more complex — seek advice from Citizens Advice.

**Temporary and exempt accommodation** — if you live in certain types of supported or temporary housing (hostels, refuges, sheltered housing with care and support), you may be able to claim Housing Benefit for your rent and Universal Credit for your other living costs at the same time. This is one of the few situations where both can run together.

**Existing claimants** — if you're already receiving Housing Benefit and haven't yet been moved to Universal Credit, you may continue to receive it until you're migrated across. Don't make a new UC claim without getting advice first if you're in this situation, as it could affect your existing entitlement.

## The UC housing costs element

If you're on Universal Credit and renting, your housing costs are covered by the housing costs element — sometimes called the housing element. This works similarly to Housing Benefit but is paid as part of your monthly UC payment rather than separately or directly to your landlord.

You can ask UC to pay your housing element directly to your landlord — this is called an Alternative Payment Arrangement and can be useful if managing a lump sum monthly payment is difficult.
