Yes — working part time doesn't stop you claiming Universal Credit. In fact, Universal Credit was specifically designed so that working doesn't mean losing all your support. Many part-time workers on low incomes are entitled to a top-up, and a surprising number never claim it.

This guide explains how it works in practice.

## How Universal Credit works alongside part-time work

Universal Credit is a single monthly payment that adjusts automatically based on what you earn. There's no cut-off point where working a certain number of hours disqualifies you — what matters is your overall income and circumstances, not your job title or contract type.

You can work full time, part time, be on a zero-hours contract, or be self-employed and still potentially qualify. The system is built to taper your payment down gradually as your earnings rise, rather than stopping it abruptly the moment you start work.

## How your earnings affect your payment

Once you start earning above a certain point, your Universal Credit payment reduces gradually rather than stopping outright. For every pound you earn above your relevant threshold, your payment is reduced by 55 pence. This means you always keep some benefit from working more hours — you're never worse off overall for taking on extra shifts.

Your savings matter too. If you and your partner have £16,000 or more in savings between you, you generally won't be eligible. Between £6,000 and £16,000, a smaller deduction applies. Below £6,000, your savings don't affect your claim at all.

## Work allowances explained

If you have children, or a health condition that limits your ability to work, you may get a "work allowance" — an amount you can earn before the taper even starts. This means your first slice of part-time earnings is yours to keep in full before any reduction kicks in.



## How to check what you could get

Because the calculation depends on your exact hours, pay, housing costs, and household circumstances, the only reliable way to know what you'd actually receive is to run the numbers for your situation.
