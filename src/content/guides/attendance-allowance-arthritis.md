Arthritis is one of the most common conditions among Attendance Allowance claimants — yet many older people with severe arthritis assume their condition isn't bad enough to qualify. If arthritis significantly affects your ability to wash, dress, or carry out personal care safely, you may well be entitled.

## What is Attendance Allowance?

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free, non-means-tested benefit for people over State Pension age who need help with personal care or supervision due to disability or illness. It pays £73.90/week (lower rate) or £110.40/week (higher rate) in 2026/27.

## How arthritis affects Attendance Allowance eligibility

Arthritis — whether rheumatoid, osteoarthritis, or other types — can affect multiple aspects of personal care:

**Hand and upper limb arthritis:**
- Difficulty gripping and twisting — taps, jar lids, buttons, zips
- Pain and weakness when lifting — pots, cups, plates
- Difficulty washing hair, reaching to wash back and feet
- Problems fastening clothing, managing buttons and zips

**Hip and knee arthritis:**
- Getting in and out of the bath becomes difficult and risky
- Sitting down and standing up from the toilet
- Difficulty bending to wash lower legs and feet
- Risk of falls when moving between positions

**General effects:**
- Morning stiffness — many people with arthritis need help in the morning before joints loosen up
- Fatigue — particularly relevant in rheumatoid arthritis
- Pain that makes routine tasks slow, difficult, and exhausting

## The safety dimension

Falls are a significant risk for older people with arthritis, particularly in the bathroom. If arthritis means you need someone present when bathing or showering to prevent falls, this counts as supervision for Attendance Allowance purposes even if they're not physically helping.

## Which rate applies to arthritis?

Arthritis affecting primarily daytime personal care typically attracts the lower rate. If night-time needs are also established — for example, needing help to turn in bed due to pain, getting up to use the toilet at night, or needing help after a fall at night — the higher rate may apply.

## Rheumatoid arthritis and fluctuation

Rheumatoid arthritis is a fluctuating condition with flares and better periods. The Attendance Allowance assessment should reflect needs across the realistic range, including during flares. If flares significantly affect care needs for significant periods, describe this clearly in the claim.

## What evidence helps the claim

- GP letter describing the arthritis, affected joints, severity, and impact on personal care
- Rheumatologist letter if under specialist care for rheumatoid arthritis
- Occupational therapy assessment if one has been done
- Description of specific tasks that need help and why
- Details of any aids and adaptations already in use (grab rails, bath board, dressing aids)

## What Attendance Allowance unlocks

Attendance Allowance can trigger or increase Pension Credit through the severe disability addition, unlocking Council Tax Reduction, free NHS dental treatment, and other passported benefits. Arthritis Action and Versus Arthritis both have benefits advice services.
