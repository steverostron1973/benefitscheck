Heart conditions including heart failure, severe angina, and other serious cardiac conditions can qualify for Attendance Allowance where they significantly affect personal care or safety. Many people with serious heart conditions don't realise they may be entitled to this support.

## What is Attendance Allowance?

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free benefit for people over State Pension age who need help with personal care or supervision due to a physical or mental disability. It pays £73.90/week (lower rate) or £110.40/week (higher rate) in 2026/27. Income and savings don't affect eligibility.

## How heart conditions affect Attendance Allowance eligibility

The key question for Attendance Allowance is whether the condition means you need help with personal care or supervision to keep you safe. For heart conditions, several factors are relevant:

**Breathlessness and fatigue:**
Heart failure and other serious cardiac conditions cause breathlessness and fatigue that can significantly affect the ability to wash, dress, and carry out personal care. If exertion causes breathlessness that makes bathing, showering, or dressing exhausting or unsafe, this is directly relevant.

**Safety supervision:**
Some heart conditions create safety risks that mean supervision is needed. If someone has unpredictable symptoms — for example, blackouts, severe breathlessness episodes, or falls due to their condition — supervision to keep them safe counts toward Attendance Allowance.

**Night-time needs:**
Heart failure often causes breathlessness that's worse lying flat, disturbed sleep, and night-time symptoms that may require help. These count toward the higher rate.

**Mobility effects:**
While Attendance Allowance doesn't have a mobility component (unlike PIP), mobility limitations caused by a cardiac condition often also affect personal care — if you can't walk far without breathlessness, getting in and out of the bath becomes difficult or unsafe.

## Terminal illness and Attendance Allowance

If a doctor has confirmed that a heart condition is terminal and the person isn't expected to live more than 12 months, they automatically qualify for the higher rate of Attendance Allowance under the Special Rules for Terminal Illness. The claim can be made by phone and processed quickly — usually within days.

## Which rate applies to heart conditions?

This depends on the severity of the condition and how it affects care needs. Someone with well-controlled heart disease and minimal functional impact may not meet the threshold. Someone with severe heart failure causing significant breathlessness, fatigue, and night-time symptoms may qualify for the higher rate.

## What evidence helps the claim

- GP letter describing the cardiac condition, severity, and functional impact on daily living
- Cardiologist letter if under specialist care
- Details of any hospital admissions related to the condition
- Description of specific care tasks that are difficult or need help due to breathlessness or fatigue
- Night-time needs described in detail

## What Attendance Allowance unlocks

Attendance Allowance can trigger or increase Pension Credit through the severe disability addition, which unlocks Council Tax Reduction, help with NHS costs, and other passported benefits. British Heart Foundation has a benefits advice service that can help with claims.
