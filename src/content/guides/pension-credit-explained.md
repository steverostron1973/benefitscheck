Pension Credit is one of the most valuable and most underclaimed benefits in the UK. It's estimated that around £2 billion in Pension Credit goes unclaimed every year — not because people aren't entitled, but because they don't know they are, or don't realise how much it can unlock.

## What is Pension Credit?

Pension Credit is a means-tested benefit for people over State Pension age on a low income. It comes in two parts:

**Guarantee Credit** tops up your weekly income to a minimum of £227.10 if you're single, or £346.60 if you're a couple (2026/27 rates). If your income is below these amounts, Guarantee Credit makes up the difference.

**Savings Credit** is an additional amount for people who saved toward their retirement. It's only available to people who reached State Pension age before 6 April 2016.

## Why it's so valuable beyond the payment itself

Claiming Pension Credit — even a small amount — acts as a passport to a range of other benefits:

- Free TV licence (if you're 75 or over)
- Full Council Tax Reduction
- Free NHS dental treatment
- Help with NHS costs (glasses, hospital travel)
- Cold Weather Payment
- Housing Benefit (if renting)
- Warm Home Discount

The total value of these passported benefits can far exceed the Pension Credit payment itself — which is why it's worth claiming even if you think you're only entitled to a small top-up.

## Common reasons people don't claim

Many pensioners assume they won't qualify because they have savings or a private pension. But Pension Credit doesn't have a hard savings limit in the same way UC does — savings affect the calculation but don't automatically disqualify you. Someone with modest savings and a small private pension may still be entitled.

Others feel it's not worth the effort for a small amount — but given the passported benefits, even £1 of Pension Credit entitlement can unlock thousands of pounds of other support.

## How to claim

Call the Pension Credit claim line on 0800 99 1234 (free to call, Monday to Friday 8am to 6pm). You can also claim online through GOV.UK. Claims can be backdated by up to three months.
