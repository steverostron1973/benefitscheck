Parkinson's disease is one of the conditions most commonly associated with Attendance Allowance claims, and most people with moderate to advanced Parkinson's will qualify — often at the higher rate. Yet many people with Parkinson's and their carers don't claim until the condition is already significantly advanced. Claiming earlier means receiving support for longer.

## What is Attendance Allowance?

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free, non-means-tested benefit for people over State Pension age who need help with personal care or supervision due to disability or illness. It pays either £73.90/week (lower rate) or £110.40/week (higher rate) in 2026/27.

## How Parkinson's affects Attendance Allowance eligibility

Parkinson's causes a progressive range of symptoms that increasingly affect personal care and safety:

**Motor symptoms affecting personal care:**
- Tremor makes tasks like shaving, applying makeup, fastening buttons, and using cutlery difficult or impossible
- Rigidity and slowness (bradykinesia) make dressing, washing, and bathing slow and exhausting
- Balance problems increase the risk of falls, particularly in the bathroom
- Freezing episodes — where movement suddenly stops — create significant safety risks

**Non-motor symptoms:**
- Fatigue is a major feature of Parkinson's and affects the ability to complete personal care tasks
- Cognitive changes and dementia (which affects many people with Parkinson's in later stages) add supervision needs
- Speech difficulties can affect the ability to communicate needs
- Sleep disturbance and night-time symptoms including vivid dreams and restless legs can create night-time care needs

## Which rate does Parkinson's usually attract?

Early Parkinson's with mild symptoms may qualify for the lower rate. As the condition progresses and symptoms affect both day and night — which is very common in Parkinson's due to night-time movement difficulties, sleep disturbance, and needing help to turn in bed or reach the toilet — the higher rate becomes appropriate.

Many people with moderate to advanced Parkinson's qualify for the higher rate.

## The progressive nature of Parkinson's and claiming early

Because Parkinson's is progressive, people are sometimes advised to wait until they "really need it" before claiming. This is a mistake — Attendance Allowance is paid from the date of claim, not backdated to when the condition started. Claiming as soon as the criteria are met means receiving the benefit for longer.

If awarded at the lower rate initially, you can request a reassessment when needs increase to the higher rate level.

## What evidence helps the claim

- GP letter describing Parkinson's diagnosis, current stage, and functional impact
- Neurologist letter if under specialist care
- Parkinson's nurse letter if applicable — many areas have specialist Parkinson's nurses whose input carries significant weight
- Description of specific care tasks needed and how long they take
- Details of any falls or safety incidents

Parkinson's UK has an excellent benefits advice service and can help with completing the Attendance Allowance form.

## What Attendance Allowance unlocks

Like all Attendance Allowance awards, receiving it can trigger or increase Pension Credit through the severe disability addition, unlocking further benefits including Council Tax Reduction and NHS cost help.
