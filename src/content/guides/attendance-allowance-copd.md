COPD (chronic obstructive pulmonary disease) and other serious respiratory conditions can significantly affect personal care and safety in older age. Many people with severe breathing conditions qualify for Attendance Allowance but don't claim because they don't realise the connection between breathlessness and care needs.

## What is Attendance Allowance?

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free benefit for people over State Pension age who need help with personal care or supervision due to a physical or mental disability. It pays £73.90/week (lower rate) or £110.40/week (higher rate) in 2026/27. Income and savings don't affect eligibility.

## How COPD and breathing conditions affect Attendance Allowance

The connection between breathing conditions and personal care is often overlooked. Breathlessness on exertion directly affects many personal care activities:

**Washing and bathing:**
Getting in and out of the bath, showering, and washing hair all require significant physical effort. For someone with severe COPD, these activities can cause dangerous levels of breathlessness. Many people with severe COPD cannot safely bathe or shower without help or supervision.

**Dressing:**
Bending to put on shoes and socks, reaching to dress upper body, and the exertion of getting dressed can all cause breathlessness in severe COPD.

**Safety supervision:**
If breathlessness could become severe enough to require emergency help during personal care, supervision needs count toward Attendance Allowance.

**Night-time needs:**
COPD often causes night-time breathlessness, coughing, and disturbed sleep. If someone regularly needs help or attention during the night due to their breathing condition, this counts toward the higher rate.

## Oxygen therapy and Attendance Allowance

If someone uses supplemental oxygen, this doesn't automatically qualify them for Attendance Allowance — but managing oxygen equipment, and the care needs associated with needing oxygen, are relevant to the assessment.

## Terminal illness

If a respiratory condition is terminal and a doctor confirms the person isn't expected to live more than 12 months, they automatically qualify for higher rate Attendance Allowance under the Special Rules for Terminal Illness, with fast-track processing.

## Which rate applies to COPD?

Mild to moderate COPD that's well managed may not meet the threshold. Severe COPD causing significant breathlessness on minimal exertion, affecting personal care and creating night-time needs, may qualify for the higher rate.

## What evidence helps the claim

- GP letter describing the diagnosis, severity (FEV1 readings and COPD stage if relevant), and functional impact
- Respiratory specialist letter if under one
- Details of any hospital admissions for the condition
- Description of specific personal care activities affected by breathlessness
- Night-time needs described clearly

## What Attendance Allowance unlocks

Attendance Allowance can trigger or increase Pension Credit, unlocking Council Tax Reduction, NHS cost help, and other passported benefits. The British Lung Foundation has a helpline and benefits advice resources.
