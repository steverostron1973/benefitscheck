One of the most persistent myths about PIP is that you can't claim it if you're working — or that working full time somehow proves you don't have a disability. Neither is true. PIP is one of the few benefits in the UK system that is completely unaffected by your employment status or income.

## Why work doesn't affect PIP

PIP — Personal Independence Payment — is assessed solely on how a health condition or disability affects your ability to carry out everyday activities and get around. It has no earnings limit, no hours limit, and no means test.

Whether you work 4 hours a week or 50 hours a week, your PIP entitlement is determined entirely by your functional ability — not your job status. Someone who manages to work full time through significant effort, pain, or with workplace adjustments can be just as entitled to PIP as someone who can't work at all.

## The "you must be very disabled to work" misconception

Assessors are specifically trained not to use employment as evidence against a claim. The fact that you work doesn't mean your condition doesn't affect you — it may mean you're pushing through significant difficulty, relying on aids and adaptations, or struggling with daily tasks outside of work hours.

What matters is how your condition affects you across all 12 PIP activities — things like preparing food, washing and bathing, managing medication, and planning journeys — not whether you hold down a job.

## What working claimants should focus on

If you're working and claiming PIP, describe how your condition affects you across all parts of your life — not just at work. Focus on:

- How long tasks take you compared to someone without your condition
- Whether you need aids, adaptations, or another person's help
- How you are on bad days, not just good days
- The cumulative effect of your condition throughout the day
