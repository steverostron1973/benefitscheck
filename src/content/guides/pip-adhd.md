ADHD is increasingly recognised in PIP assessments for adults, but many people with ADHD don't claim because they assume it's not severe enough or only affects their work. In reality, ADHD can significantly affect daily living activities — particularly those involving planning, organisation, memory, and managing risk.

## ADHD and PIP — what assessors look for

PIP doesn't assess whether you have ADHD; it assesses whether ADHD affects your ability to carry out specific daily activities safely, to an acceptable standard, repeatedly, and in a reasonable time. Executive function difficulties, impulsivity, and attention problems can all affect multiple PIP activities.

## How ADHD typically affects PIP activities

**Daily Living activities often affected:**

- **Preparing food** — impulsivity and inattention can create genuine safety risks in the kitchen (leaving hobs on, forgetting food cooking, using sharp implements unsafely). This is one of the most important areas for ADHD claims.
- **Managing medication** — difficulty with routine and memory can make managing a consistent medication regime genuinely challenging
- **Making budgeting decisions** — impulsive spending and difficulty managing finances are common in ADHD and this activity specifically covers the ability to manage money safely
- **Planning and following journeys** — difficulty with planning, time management, and staying on task can affect the ability to follow unfamiliar routes independently

**Mobility activities often affected:**

- **Planning and following journeys** — as above, executive function difficulties and impulsivity can affect the ability to plan and follow journeys safely, particularly in unfamiliar or complex environments

## The safety criterion — particularly relevant for ADHD

The safety criterion asks whether you can complete an activity without risk of harm. For ADHD, this is particularly relevant in the kitchen — impulsivity, inattention, and distraction create genuine risks when cooking. If you've had incidents (burns, fires, flooding) or consistently need reminders or supervision when cooking, this should be described clearly.

## What award might you expect?

ADHD awards vary significantly. Well-managed ADHD with minimal functional impact may not meet the threshold. ADHD with significant executive function difficulties affecting multiple daily activities — particularly financial management, medication management, and journey planning — may qualify for Standard or Enhanced Daily Living, and in some cases Enhanced Mobility through the journey planning descriptor.

## Claiming while working

PIP is completely unaffected by employment. Many adults with ADHD work successfully, particularly in roles that suit their strengths, while still struggling significantly with daily living activities. Employment doesn't disqualify you.

## What evidence helps your claim

- Diagnostic assessment report for ADHD
- GP letter describing how ADHD affects your daily functioning
- Letters from a psychiatrist if you're under one
- Evidence of ADHD medication prescribed
- A supporting statement from someone who knows you well describing the support you need day to day — particularly valuable for ADHD where self-reporting limitations is genuinely difficult

## Common mistakes when claiming PIP for ADHD

**Underreporting because you've adapted** — many adults with ADHD have developed extensive coping strategies over years. This can make it hard to recognise and describe how much effort daily tasks actually take. The effort involved in coping strategies counts.

**Not mentioning safety issues** — impulsivity and inattention create real safety risks that are directly relevant to PIP scoring. Don't skip over incidents or near-misses.

**Assuming ADHD is too mild to qualify** — moderate to severe ADHD with significant executive function difficulties affecting daily activities can qualify. The threshold isn't as high as many people assume.

**Not mentioning co-occurring conditions** — ADHD frequently co-occurs with anxiety, depression, and autism. All of these can add points across additional activities and should all be included in your claim.

## PIP reviews and reassessments

ADHD is a lifelong condition, though its impact can change over time — particularly if medication changes or life circumstances shift. At reassessment, be prepared to describe how ADHD currently affects you, which may be different from how it affected you at your original assessment.
