Pension Credit has a reputation for being complicated — which is part of why so many eligible pensioners don't claim it. But the eligibility rules are actually more flexible than most people think, and the rewards for claiming can be substantial.

## The basic eligibility rule

To claim Pension Credit, you must have reached State Pension age (currently 66 for both men and women). If you have a partner, only one of you needs to have reached State Pension age for a joint claim — though both ages affect the calculation.

## The income threshold

Guarantee Credit tops up your weekly income to:
- £227.10 if you're single (2026/27)
- £346.60 if you're a couple (2026/27)

If your total weekly income from all sources — State Pension, private pension, part-time work, savings income — is below these amounts, you're likely entitled to Guarantee Credit to make up the difference.

## How savings affect your claim

Unlike Universal Credit, Pension Credit doesn't have a hard £16,000 savings cut-off. Instead, savings above £10,000 are treated as generating a notional income of £1 per week for every £500 (or part of £500) above £10,000. This notional income is added to your actual income when calculating your award.

This means that even with significant savings, you may still be entitled to a small amount of Pension Credit — and that small amount is worth claiming for the passported benefits it unlocks.

## Additional amounts

On top of the basic Guarantee Credit, you may receive additional amounts for:

- **Severe disability** — if you receive Attendance Allowance or the daily living component of PIP, and no one claims Carer's Allowance for looking after you: +£81.50/week
- **Caring** — if you receive Carer's Allowance: +£45.60/week
- **Housing costs** — certain mortgage interest costs can be covered

## Savings Credit

If you reached State Pension age before 6 April 2016, you may also be entitled to Savings Credit — an additional amount that rewards people who saved toward their retirement. The maximum is £17.01/week for a single person, £19.04/week for a couple.

## How to claim

Call 0800 99 1234 (free, Monday to Friday 8am to 6pm) or apply online through GOV.UK. Claims can be backdated by up to three months — so if you think you've been entitled for a while, apply now and request backdating.
