If you live alone, you're already entitled to a 25% single person discount on your council tax bill. But if you're also on a low income, you may be able to reduce it much further through Council Tax Reduction — potentially to zero.

## The single person discount

First things first: if you're the only adult in your home, make sure you're receiving the 25% single person discount. Contact your local council if you're not — it applies automatically in most cases but is sometimes missed after a change in circumstances.

## Council Tax Reduction on top of the discount

Council Tax Reduction is calculated on your bill after the single person discount is applied. So if your full bill is £1,800/year and you receive the 25% discount, your reduced bill is £1,350 — and CTR is then calculated as a percentage of that.

## How much could you save?

This depends entirely on your local council's scheme. Pensioners are protected by a national standard and can receive up to 100% CTR — meaning a zero council tax bill. For working-age people, councils set their own rules, but many offer 80-100% reduction for people on very low incomes or those receiving certain benefits.

## What affects your entitlement

The main factors councils consider are:

- Your weekly income (earnings, benefits, pension)
- Your savings (most councils apply a £6,000-£16,000 capital threshold)
- Whether you receive certain qualifying benefits (being on UC or Income Support often increases your CTR)
- Your council tax band and the local CTR scheme rules

## Apply as soon as your circumstances change

CTR is not automatically backdated. If your income drops, you become unemployed, or you move to a new area, apply for CTR immediately — most councils will only backdate by a matter of weeks, if at all.
