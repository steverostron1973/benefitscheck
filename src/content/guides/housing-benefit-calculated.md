If you're eligible for Housing Benefit, the amount you receive isn't necessarily your full rent. It's calculated based on several factors — and understanding how can help you plan your finances and avoid shortfalls.

## Local Housing Allowance (LHA)

For private renters, the maximum Housing Benefit you can receive is capped at the Local Housing Allowance rate for your area. LHA rates are set by the government based on the lower end of local private rental market prices — specifically the 30th percentile of rents in your Broad Rental Market Area.

LHA rates vary significantly across the country. Rates in London and the South East are higher than in the North, but even so, they often fall short of actual market rents in those areas.

## Household size and the bedroom cap

Your LHA rate is based on the number of bedrooms your household is entitled to, not the size of your actual property. The rules are:

- Single person under 35: entitled to the shared accommodation rate only (covering a room in a shared house, not a whole property)
- Single person 35 or over: one bedroom
- Couple with no children: one bedroom
- Additional bedrooms for children (with some sharing rules for children under 10 and under 16)

If you rent a property larger than your entitlement, you'll only receive the LHA rate for the size you're entitled to.

## Income taper

Housing Benefit reduces as your income rises. The taper rate means that for every £1 of net income above a certain threshold, your Housing Benefit reduces by 65p. This means working while claiming Housing Benefit (where still eligible) always leaves you better off overall.

## Savings

If you have savings of £16,000 or more, you're not eligible for Housing Benefit. Between £6,000 and £16,000, a tariff income is applied which reduces your award.

## The benefit cap

Total benefits including Housing Benefit are subject to an overall benefit cap — £442.31/week for families outside London, £526.29/week for families in London (2026/27). If your total benefits exceed this cap, Housing Benefit is usually the payment that's reduced first.
