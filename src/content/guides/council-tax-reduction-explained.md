Council Tax Reduction (CTR) — sometimes still called Council Tax Benefit — is a discount on your council tax bill based on your income and circumstances. Unlike most benefits, it's administered locally, meaning the rules vary depending on where you live.

## Who can apply

Anyone on a low income can apply for Council Tax Reduction, whether they're working or not. You don't need to be claiming other benefits to qualify. The key factors are:

- Your income and your partner's income (if applicable)
- Your savings and capital
- Who lives in your household
- Your council tax bill amount

Pensioners are protected by a national minimum standard — they can receive up to 100% reduction. Working-age rules vary by council, but most offer significant reductions for people on low incomes.

## How much you could get

The reduction can range from a small discount to your entire council tax bill being wiped out. Some councils offer 100% reduction for people on very low incomes; others cap it at a lower percentage. The only way to know your specific entitlement is to apply to your local council.

## The savings limit

Most councils apply a savings limit — if you have more than £16,000 in capital, you generally won't qualify. Between £6,000 and £16,000, a tariff income is applied which gradually reduces your entitlement.

## How to apply

Apply directly to your local council — most have an online application. Apply as soon as your circumstances change, since CTR is generally only backdated in limited circumstances. If you're also claiming Universal Credit, tell your council straight away as they'll need to know.
