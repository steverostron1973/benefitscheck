The UK benefits system sees significant changes almost every April, and 2026 is no exception. Whether you're currently claiming or thinking about making a claim, here's what's changed and what it means for you.

## Universal Credit health element cuts

One of the most significant changes for 2026 affects new claimants with limited capability for work. The UC health element (LCWRA addition) is being reduced for new claimants from April 2026, while existing claimants are protected. If you're making a new UC claim on health grounds, the additional amount you receive will be lower than it was previously — making it more important than ever to also check PIP entitlement, which remains unchanged.

## PIP assessment changes

The government announced reforms to PIP assessments, with changes to how certain activities are scored — particularly around daily living activities for people with mental health conditions and neurodivergent conditions. If you're due for a PIP review or making a new claim, it's worth understanding how the current descriptors apply to your condition before your assessment.

## Benefit uprating — April 2026

Most benefits rose by 1.7% in April 2026, in line with the September 2025 CPI inflation figure. This affects:

- Universal Credit standard allowance and elements
- PIP daily living and mobility rates
- Carer's Allowance
- New Style ESA and JSA
- Pension Credit

The State Pension increased by 4.1% under the triple lock (the highest of earnings growth, inflation, or 2.5%).

## Childcare expansion

The government's expanded free childcare entitlement — offering up to 30 hours per week for children aged 9 months to 4 years for working parents — has been fully rolled out. If you have a child under 4 and you're working, check your entitlement through your local council or the Childcare Choices website.

## Household Support Fund

The Household Support Fund, which provides discretionary support through local councils for essentials like food and energy, has been extended again. Contact your local council to find out what's available in your area — eligibility and what's on offer varies significantly by location.

## Benefit cap uprating

The benefit cap increased slightly in April 2026 in line with inflation. The new limits are £442.31 per week for families outside London and £526.29 per week for families in London (£384.62 and £457.97 per week respectively for single people without children).
